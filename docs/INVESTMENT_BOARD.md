# Investment Board

The Investment Board is the governance gate between validation and execution.

It decides whether a product, SaaS, app, business, or major automation idea should receive company execution capacity.

## Decision Options

- `GO` — build the project.
- `PIVOT` — modify the idea before building.
- `RESEARCH_MORE` — continue analysis before deciding.
- `REJECT` — do not build for now.

## Required Members

- COO / Operations Manager
- Product Manager
- Research Analyst
- Finance / Business Analyst
- Marketing Strategist
- UX Designer
- Chief Architect
- Security Analyst, when relevant
- Investment Board Chair

The Board may include Portfolio Manager, Innovation Manager, Venture Analyst, QA Analyst, Customer Success Manager, Documentation Specialist, or Automation Specialist when their judgment affects the decision.

## Board Responsibilities

- Consolidate evidence from Challenge & Validation.
- Challenge assumptions and enthusiasm bias.
- Compare opportunity quality against complexity and risk.
- Decide whether execution should start.
- Document why the idea was accepted, modified, delayed, or rejected.

## Decision Rules

- `GO` requires a clear problem, credible target customer, plausible willingness to pay or strategic value, manageable risk, and a realistic MVP.
- `PIVOT` is used when the problem is real but the proposed solution, market, customer, or scope needs correction.
- `RESEARCH_MORE` is used when decision-critical evidence is missing.
- `REJECT` is used when pain, market, differentiation, economics, strategic alignment, or execution feasibility is too weak for current investment.

## Anti-Bias Rule

The Board must explicitly guard against "everything should be built" bias.

The Board Chair must force a clear decision and may reject vague roadmaps that skip validation.
