# Idea Intake Process

Idea Intake converts a vague CEO request into a structured opportunity brief.

It should be lightweight. The purpose is to understand the idea well enough to validate it, not to design the full product.

## Trigger Examples

- "I have an app idea for real estate brokers."
- "Could we build a SaaS for accountants?"
- "I want to automate operations for my agency."
- "There might be a product in this workflow."

## Intake Steps

```txt
1. Capture the raw idea.
2. Restate it in plain language.
3. Identify the likely problem.
4. Identify the likely buyer and user.
5. Capture why this matters now.
6. Identify business objective.
7. List assumptions.
8. List constraints.
9. Decide what clarification is truly needed.
10. Route to Challenge & Validation.
```

## Minimal Clarification

Ask questions only when the answer changes the validation path.

Good clarification questions:

- Who is the target customer?
- What painful workflow are they dealing with today?
- Is the goal revenue, internal efficiency, strategic positioning, or learning?
- Are there constraints on budget, timeline, geography, compliance, or platform?

Avoid broad discovery interviews when a first-pass validation can proceed with stated assumptions.
