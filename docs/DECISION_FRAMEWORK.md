# Decision Framework

OfficeCorp uses a simple 1 to 5 scoring model for product, SaaS, app, business, and major automation ideas.

The score supports judgment. It does not replace judgment.

## Scoring Categories

| Category | 1 means | 5 means |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Market Pain | weak or speculative pain | urgent, frequent, costly pain |
| Revenue Potential | unclear or tiny monetization | strong willingness to pay or strategic value |
| Strategic Alignment | distracts from CEO goals | strongly advances CEO goals |
| Differentiation | commodity or clone | clear and defensible angle |
| Execution Complexity | very complex | simple to execute |
| Acquisition Difficulty | very hard to reach buyers | accessible, clear channels |
| Risk Level | high risk | low risk |
| Speed to MVP | slow and uncertain | fast to test |

## Inverted Scores

Complexity, acquisition difficulty, and risk are scored as execution attractiveness, not raw danger.

That means:

- `Execution Complexity = 5` means low complexity.
- `Acquisition Difficulty = 5` means customers are easy to reach.
- `Risk Level = 5` means low risk.

Do not average raw risk, raw difficulty, or raw complexity without inversion.

## Recommendation Bands

| Average score | Recommendation |
| --- | --- |
| 4.0+ | Strong candidate |
| 3.0-3.9 | Promising but needs refinement |
| 2.0-2.9 | Weak or risky |
| < 2.0 | Reject or archive |

## Decision Mapping

- Strong candidate: likely `GO`, unless a veto risk exists.
- Promising but needs refinement: likely `PIVOT` or focused `RESEARCH_MORE`.
- Weak or risky: likely `RESEARCH_MORE`, `PIVOT`, or `REJECT`.
- Reject or archive: likely `REJECT`.

## Required Caveat

If market evidence is weak, the report must label scores as hypothesis scores and recommend the smallest research step that would improve confidence.
