# Challenge & Validation Phase

The Challenge & Validation Phase is mandatory before OfficeCorp commits engineering effort to a meaningful product, SaaS, app, business, or major automation idea.

The goal is not to discourage building. The goal is to protect the CEO from building the wrong thing too early.

## Trigger

Run this phase when the CEO expresses:

- a new product idea;
- a SaaS idea;
- an app idea;
- a business opportunity;
- a significant automation initiative;
- a vague entrepreneurial need that could become a product or venture.

## Core Questions

The phase must answer:

- Is the problem real?
- Who has this problem?
- Is the problem painful enough to pay for?
- What alternatives or competitors already exist?
- What differentiation is plausible?
- Is the idea aligned with the CEO's business goals?
- What is the revenue potential?
- How complex is development?
- What are the main risks?
- Should OfficeCorp recommend `GO`, `PIVOT`, `RESEARCH_MORE`, or `REJECT`?

## Required Work

```txt
1. Intake
2. Minimal clarification
3. Problem statement
4. Target customer hypothesis
5. Market and competitor scan
6. Business model hypothesis
7. Risk analysis
8. Scoring
9. Investment Board report
```

Minimal clarification means OfficeCorp asks only what is needed to avoid analyzing the wrong idea. If the CEO provides little detail, OfficeCorp should state assumptions and proceed with a lightweight first pass.

## Agent Contributions

- Innovation Manager: reformulates the raw idea and identifies hidden angles.
- Product Manager: defines user problem, MVP logic, and product strategy implications.
- Research Analyst: verifies market and competitor facts when freshness matters.
- Venture Analyst: evaluates market, monetization, acquisition, and venture quality.
- Finance / Business Analyst: evaluates pricing, revenue model, cost, and profitability.
- Marketing Strategist: evaluates positioning and acquisition channels.
- UX Designer: evaluates user workflow, usability, and adoption friction.
- Chief Architect: estimates technical complexity and execution tradeoffs.
- Security Analyst: joins when the idea touches sensitive data, regulated domains, auth, permissions, payments, or trust.
- Investment Board Chair: consolidates the decision and prevents build-by-default bias.

## Output

The output must be decision-ready:

```txt
Idea:
Problem:
Target customer:
Evidence:
Competitors:
Differentiation:
Business model:
Scores:
Risks:
Board recommendation:
Next steps:
```

If evidence is weak, the output must say so and recommend targeted research rather than pretending confidence.
